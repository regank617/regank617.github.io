About Me

I am currently a researcher under Dr. Gonzalo R. Arce, working to bridge the gap between academia and industry. My focus is on biomedical machine learning and integrating it with mass spectrometry to solve real-world safety problems in hospitals and public transit.

I am a recipient of the NIH T32 pre-doctoral training award and have collaborated with partners like **Zeteo Tech Inc.** and **Johns Hopkins Hospital**. Beyond research, I am passionate about mentoring first-semester graduate students and teaching data science principles.

Key Research Projects

Zeteo Tech Collab

Environmental Pathogen Monitoring

Translating mass spectrometry from the lab to the field. Using autonomous air collection and advanced sensing models to detect harmful pathogens (like E. coli) in airports and subways with minimal sample prep.

#FieldTesting #PublicSafety #BioDefense
Johns Hopkins Collab

Breath Diagnostics (Generative AI)

Developing noninvasive technology to detect lung disease directly from breath. I am preparing a first-author paper on novel improvements to generative AI for infection diagnostics.

#GenerativeAI #HealthDiagnostics #ClinicalApplication
Algorithm Design

Transformer Models & Tokenizers

Engineered a novel efficient tokenizer based on next-wavelet prediction. Built transformer-based models guided by biological priors, boosting microbial classification F1 scores from 82.4% to 98.3%.

#Transformers #Wavelets #DeepLearning
Leadership

Data Science Teaching

Leading undergraduate teams on semester-long data science projects. Teaching core principles of data collection, ML applications, and presentation skills while serving as a mentor for first-year Ph.D. students.
